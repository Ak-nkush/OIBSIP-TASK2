Stanley Martin Lieber

(1922 - 2018)

Creator of Marvel Universe
About

Born on December 28, 1922, in New York City, Stan Lee, the legendary co-creator of Marvel Comics' most iconic superheroes, was a true titan of imagination. With his boundless creativity and charismatic personality, he revolutionized the comic book industry and forever altered the landscape of pop culture. From Spider-Man's friendly neighborhood heroics to the cosmic adventures of the X-Men, Lee's characters not only entertained but also reflected the complexities of the human condition. His famous catchphrase,"Excelsior," encapsulates his unwavering passion for storytelling and his belief in the extraordinary potential within each of us. Lee continued to inspire generations of fans and creators until his passing on November 12, 2018, leaving behind a legacy that proves that with great imagination comes great power.
